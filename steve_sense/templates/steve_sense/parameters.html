Enables the light rail function


'Rail Start' specifies what time the light rail will start moving. 'Duration' dictates how long it should take to reach its end-point.


The 'Auto-Sync Light Rail' feature will start the light rail when the light is first powered and scale the speed until it reaches its end-point when the light goes off.

Enables the Heating function.


'Pulse Width' Is the amount of time the device will be powered on in the total 'Time Period'.


'Target Temp' is the temperature the system will try to maintain. 'Target Deviation' is the allowable deviation from this target.

For Example: 25° Target with 2.5°c Deviation will create a range of 2.5° below the target, in the case of heating and 2.5°c above the target in the case of cooling.
The system will try keep the temperature within this range, ie: 22.5 - 25

'Pulse Width' Is the amount of time the device will be powered on in the total 'Time Period'.

The 'Intensity Timing Pattern' is created by entering a minimum of 3 Time & intensity(%) values

'Exterior-Differential Analysis' will use air from outside to replace unfavourable air inside based on certain parameters.
This is independant to VPD targeting and its not reccommended to run them concurrently.


'Targets' are what the system will try and acheive by removing air.


'Target Deviations' are the allowable deviations from the set targets.

A deviation of 2.5°c on a Target of 25°c will result in a buffer either side of 25 equal to the deviation amount.
The system will correct to within these limits.


The 'Temperature/Humidity Bias' applies a bias to either Target Temperature Deviation Limits or to Target Humidity Deviation Limits to favour a correction of one over the other.


The 'Correction Priority' is the priority given to the Exterior-Differential correction versus other corrections, expressed simply as a duration (HH:MM) for how long the Exterior-Differential Analysis will run before allowing other corrections to step in.
